Slide 1: Introduction to World War I
World War I, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict that lasted from 1914 to 1918.
It involved many of the world's nations and was centered in Europe.
The war was triggered by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria.

Slide 2: Causes of World War I
The war was caused by a complex set of factors including:
- Nationalism: Intense national pride and competition among European nations.
- Imperialism: Competition for colonies and resources.
- Militarism: The buildup of military forces and arms race.
- Alliances: Entangling alliances between major powers, including the Triple Entente and the Triple Alliance.
- Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand: The immediate spark that ignited the conflict.

Slide 3: Major Battles and Fronts
Key battles and fronts of World War I included:
- Western Front: Characterized by trench warfare and major battles such as the Battle of the Somme and the Battle of Verdun.
- Eastern Front: Involvement of Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Russia with major battles like the Battle of Tannenberg.
- Other Fronts: Fighting in the Middle East, Africa, and Asia.
- Notable battles: The Battle of the Marne, the Battle of Gallipoli, and the Battle of the Argonne Forest.

Slide 4: Consequences and Legacy of World War I
The aftermath of World War I had profound effects on the world:
- Treaty of Versailles: The peace treaty that ended the war, imposing heavy reparations and territorial losses on Germany.
- Political Changes: The collapse of empires (Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian, and German) and the rise of new nations.
- Social and Economic Impact: Significant loss of life, economic hardship, and the shaping of modern geopolitics.
- Prelude to World War II: The unresolved issues and consequences of World War I set the stage for World War II.
